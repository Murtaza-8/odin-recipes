Chicken Biryani


Description:

Chicken biryani is a delicious Pakistani/Indian rice dish that's typically reserved for special occasions such as weddings, parties, or holidays such as Ramadan. It has a lengthy preparation, but the work is definitely worth it. For biryani, basmati rice is the ideal variety to use. Chicken Biryani is a flavorful South Asian rice dish made with fragrant basmati rice, tender chicken, and a blend of aromatic spices like cumin, cardamom, and cloves. Layered with fried onions, fresh herbs, and sometimes saffron, it has a rich, savory taste with hints of spice and warmth. Often served with raita or salad, this dish is loved for its vibrant aroma, colorful presentation, and perfect balance of flavors.

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 4 small potatoes, peeled and halved
  • 2 large onions, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon minced fresh ginger root
  • 2 medium tomatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 2 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh mint leaves
  • ½ teaspoon ground cardamom
  • 1 (2 inch) piece cinnamon stick
  • 3 pounds boneless, skinless chicken pieces cut into chunks

Steps

1. Fry Potatoes

  • Heat 2 tbsp oil in a skillet.
  • Fry potato pieces until lightly browned (3–5 minutes).
  • Remove and place on a paper towel to drain.

2. Prepare Chicken Masala

  • Add 2 tbsp oil to skillet.
  • Sauté onions, garlic, and fresh ginger until soft and golden.
  • Add tomatoes, salt, cumin, chili powder, pepper, and turmeric; cook 5 minutes.
  • Stir in yogurt, mint, ground cardamom, and a cinnamon stick. Cook on low until tomatoes become pulp (add hot water if too dry).
  • Add chicken pieces, coat well, and cook covered on very low heat (35–45 minutes) until tender, with a thick gravy left.

3. Prepare Rice

  • Wash rice thoroughly and drain in a colander for 30 minutes.
  • In a skillet, heat oil and sauté until golden.
  • Add cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, ground ginger, and saffron.
  • Stir in rice until coated with spices.
  • Heat stock with salt and pour over rice. Stir well.

4. Combine & Steam

  • Add the cooked chicken mixture and fried potatoes into the rice. Stir gently.
  • Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to very low.
  • Cover tightly and steam for 20 minutes without opening the lid.

5. Serve

  • Spoon biryani onto a warm serving dish and enjoy!
